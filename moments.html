Moments

Moments are all the forces acting on a platform, which make it rotate clockwise or anticlockwise.

Pivot = magnitude of force * perpendicular distance from the point it pivots about

A=Fd the forces clockwise - anticlockwise gives the moment about a point.

The point ou are taking the moment on is not included in the equation.

Moments have forces going up, and weight going down.

When the object is in uniform, we know the mass of the object is in the middle.

A rod means it does not bend. In equilibrium means the total clockwise and anticloskwise forces = 0

When moments are tilting about a position we know the points it is no longer touching is 0

Newton's second law can be used on the vertical components.
