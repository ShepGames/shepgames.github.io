Trigonometry

Tig is everything to do with triangles. We know from GCSE about S(o/h) c(a/h) t(o/a) to find different trig values.

We need to understand the graphs of trig such as cos, sin and tan. We need to know both radians and degree values.

Radians are simply values represented in terms of pi. Pi represents 180 degrees.

Don't forget your toolkit! θ must be in radians

T = triangle area = 0.5absinc

O = arclength = rθ

O = area of sector = 0.5θr^2

L = cosine rule = a^2 = ^2 + c^2 -2bcCosA

S = sin rule = a/sinA = b/sinB = c/sinC

It doesn't really corrospond to the letters, but always consider these in trig questions when calculating angles, side lengths ect...

Reciprocal trig functions

1/cosx = secx

1/sinx = cosecx

1/tanx = cot which can also be written as cosx/sinx

Trig identities

sin^2(x) + cos^2(x) = 1

tan^2(x) + 1 = sec^2(x)

sinx/cosx = tanx
