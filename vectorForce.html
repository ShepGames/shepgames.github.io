Vector Forces

Vectors are objects with both magnitude and direction.

Vectors can be used in forces questions. F = (-2i + 3j)N means a force of 2N going down, and 3 across to the right.

The key is to picture them on a a graph and draw out the forces. Newton's second law ∑F = ma is used.

Magnitude is found the same way as usual: (A^2 + B^2)

To find the direction of the magnitude we set it out like a triangle. The hypotinues is the force from A to B.

When AB = (ai + bj) tanα = ai/bj. ALWAYS DRAW IT

We want it as a bearing, so go from North, clocckwise to the point. You may need to do 360-α

A resultant force is all the forces acting on an object.
