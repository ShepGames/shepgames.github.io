Parametric Differentiation

Parametric is when you are given two equations for x and y. With these equations you will want to make a y=f(x)

style equation. We do this by creating simultaneous equations.

Sometimes you will be made to find the differentiated equation. You can find x in terms of y by subbing in x.

Example:

1)The curve is given by the parametric equations: x=t^2 and y =t(t-2) where t>=0

a)Find the co-ordinate of any pointswhere the curve meets the co-ordinate axes.

Firslty we want the main equation with x in terms of y.

x^(1/2)=t so y = (x^(1/2))((x^(1/2))-2)

Which simplifies to: y = x-2(x^(1/2))

We then find the intercepts by subbing 0 as y, then x.

0 = x-2(x^(1/2)) and y=0-2(0)

Therefore x=4 and y=0 so (4,0) and (0,0)

b)Find dy/dx in terms of x

We can differentiate both terms to get:

dy/dt = 2t-2 and dx/dt = 2t

We use the formula dy/dx=dt/dx * dy/dt

To get dt/dx we do 1/(dx/dt)

dy/dx=1/(2t) * 2t-2

=1-1/t

In terms of x: 1-1/(x)^(0.5) as t=(x)^(0.5)
