Vectors

Vectors are objects with both magnitude and direction.

Vectors are split into x and y components which make vertical and horizontal compnents. 3D vectors use an extra component.

(xi + yj) is also written as (x y) which show the directions the vector is travelling.

(xi + yj + ck) is a 3D vector.

In vectors, we assume the origin is O. With two poins A and B we can work out distances with the equation:

AB = OB - OA which means: the A to B distance is equal to the O to B distance - the O to A distance.

To work out the magnitude of a vector we use: √(A^2 + B^2)

Example:

1) Vector A lies on the position (3i + 4j)m and B on position (4i - 6j)m from the origin, O. Find the distance from A to B

Firstly we know that the vectors represent OA and OB. AB = OB - OA

(4i - 6j)-(3i + 4j) = (i -10j)m

We then want the distance so do:

Definite Integrals √(1^2 + (-10)^2)

Therefore |AB| = √(101) m
